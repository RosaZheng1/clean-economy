Carbon Capture, Utilization, and Storage (CCUS) Investment Tax Credit

The CCUS is a refundable tax credit for taxable Canadian corporations that incur eligible expenditures from 2022 to 2040.

This tax credit applies to expenditures incurred to acquire property used to capture carbon dioxide (CO2) emissions, or to transport, store, or use that captured carbon.

Qualified CCUS projects

Carbon capture, utilization, and storage (CCUS) is a suite of technologies that capture CO2 emissions from fuel combustion, industrial processes, or directly from the air, to either store the CO2 or use it in industry.

Expenditures must be for a qualified CCUS project in order to be considered eligible for the tax credit.

The expenditures must also be incurred to acquire property described in new capital cost allowance classes 57 or 58, or that is considered dual use equipment.

A qualified CCUS project must meet all of the following conditions:

  • It is expected, based on the project’s most recent project plan, to support the capture of CO2 in Canada
  • An initial project evaluation has been issued by the Minister of Natural Resources
  • Based on the most recent project plan for the project, its projected eligible use percentage equals or exceeds 10% in each of the following periods:
    • If the first project period begins after September of a calendar year, the period beginning on the first day of commercial operations and ending on December 31 of the following calendar year
    • Each calendar year of the project’s total CCUS project review period, other than a period that includes a year referred to in the previous bullet

In order to be a qualified CCUS project, it cannot be:

  • Operated to service a unit for which the commissioning date was on or before April 7, 2022 (as defined under the Reduction of Carbon Dioxide Emissions from Coal-fired Generation of Electricity Regulations)
  • Undertaken for the purpose of complying with emission standards that apply, or will apply, under the Reduction of Carbon Dioxide Emissions from Coal-fired Generation of Electricity Regulations

Initial project evaluation and verification of property

You must obtain an initial project evaluation from Natural Resources Canada for each CCUS project.

In addition, property acquired before the first day of commercial operations must be verified as being described in class 57 or 58 or as meeting the definition of dual use equipment in order to be eligible for investment tax credits.

Instructions on how to apply for the evaluation and verification

Eligible expenditures

Expenditures are eligible in proportion to the quantity of captured CO2 the project is expected to support for storage or use in eligible uses.

Eligible uses include dedicated geological storage and the use of captured carbon in producing concrete in a qualified concrete storage process. In order to be considered dedicated geological storage, a geological formation has to be capable of permanently storing captured carbon, located in a jurisdiction that was a designated jurisdiction at the time that the first qualified CCUS expenditure was made for the project, and in which no captured carbon is used for enhanced oil recovery.

Credit rates for eligible expenditures

To qualify for the investment tax credit rates, claimants must elect (in prescribed form) to meet certain labour requirements regarding prevailing wages and apprenticeship standards. Otherwise, the credit rate will be reduced by 10 percentage points.

If a claimant elects to meet the labour requirements but fails to do so, the claimant is required to pay related penalties, which may be greater if the claimant does not take corrective measures. In certain circumstances, the claimant may lose its entitlement to the credit at the regular tax credit rate.


Type of expenditure From 2022 to 2030 From 2031 to 2040
Qualified carbon capture expenditures incurred to capture carbon directly from ambient air 60% 30%
All other qualified carbon capture expenditures 50% 25%
Qualified carbon transportation, storage, or use expenditures 37.5% 18.75%

Recovery mechanism

Taxpayers must track and account for their actual use of captured CO2. If the actual eligible use percentage is lower than the projected eligible use percentage, they may have to repay credit amounts they have already received. In addition, taxpayers that dispose of property or export it from Canada may have to repay credit amounts.

How to claim the credit

You can claim the credit in the tax year in which the expenses are incurred, regardless of when the property becomes available for use.

Corporation

A corporation claiming the investment tax credit for CCUS must file Schedule 31, Investment Tax Credit – Corporations, with the return, along with a form to be made available later.

The ITC claim for CCUS is at line 200 of Schedule 31. This amount will go on line 780 of the T2 Corporation Income Tax Return.

Partnership

A partnership that allocates the investment tax credit for CCUS to the members must advise the amount of the credit allocated to each member. The partnership will have to file a form to be made available later and provide a T5013 Statement of Partnership Income to each member.

A corporation that is a member of a partnership must claim the amount of the credit allocated from the partnership on a form to be made available later and Schedule 31.

Reporting requirements

Corporations that expect to have $20 million or more of qualified CCUS expenditures over the life of the qualified CCUS project (based on the most recent project evaluation or actual expenses) will have to produce an annual Climate Risk Disclosure report beginning with the tax year in which they first claim a CCUS tax credit.

Qualified CCUS projects with qualified CCUS expenditures of $250 million or more over the life of the project (based on the most recent project evaluation or actual expenses) will have to provide knowledge sharing reports to be made publicly available.
