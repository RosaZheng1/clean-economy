Carbon Capture, Utilization, and Storage (CCUS) Investment Tax Credit (ITC)

The CCUS ITC is a refundable tax credit for taxable Canadian corporations that incur eligible expenditures from 2022 to 2040.

This ITC is related to the acquisition of property used to capture carbon dioxide (CO2) emissions from fuel combustion, industrial processes, or directly from the air, to transport, store, or use that captured carbon in industry.

Qualified CCUS projects

The CCUS ITC applies to elgible expenditures incurred for a qualified CCUS project.

A qualified CCUS project is a project that is intended to support a CCUS process by capturing CO2 that would otherwise be released into the atmosphere or directly from the ambient air, transporting captured carbon, or storing or using captured carbon.

To be considered a qualified CCUS project, it must also meet all of the following conditions:

  • Based on the project’s most recent project plan, it is expected to support the capture of CO2 in Canada for a period that is at least equal to the total CCUS project review period for the project
  • An initial project evaluation has been issued by Natural Resources Canada (NRCan)
  • Based on the project’s most recent project plan, its projected eligible use percentage equals or exceeds 10% in each of the following periods:
    • If the first project period begins after September of a calendar year, the period beginning on the first day of commercial operations and ending on December 31 of the following calendar year
    • Each calendar year of the project’s total CCUS project review period, other than a period that includes a year referred to in the previous bullet

In order to be a qualified CCUS project, it cannot be a project that is:

  • Operated to service a unit for which the commissioning date was on or before April 7, 2022
  • Undertaken for the purpose of complying with emission standards that apply, or will apply, under the Reduction of Carbon Dioxide Emissions from Coal-fired Generation of Electricity Regulations

How to apply

You must apply for, and be issued, an initial project evaluation from NRCan for each CCUS project.

In addition, property acquired before the first day of commercial operations must be verified by NRCan as being described in class 57 or 58, or as described in the definition of dual-use equipment in order to be eligible for the CCUS ITC.

How to apply with NRCan for the project evaluation and verification

Eligible expenditures

Expenditures must be for a qualified CCUS project in order to be considered eligible for the CCUS ITC.

The expenditures must also be incurred to acquire property described in new capital cost allowance classes 57 or 58, or that is described in the definition of dual-use equipment.

Qualified carbon capture and transportation expenditures are eligible in proportion to the quantity of captured CO2 the project is expected to support for storage or use in eligible uses.

Eligible uses are the storage of captured carbon in dedicated geological storage and the use of captured carbon in producing concrete in Canada or the United States using a qualified concrete storage process.

Qualified carbon storage expenditures qualify only if they are expenditures to acquire property that is expected to support storage of captured carbon solely in dedicated geological storage.

Qualified carbon use expenditures qualify only if they are expenditures to acquire property that is expected to be used to support the storage or use of captured carbon solely to produce concrete using a qualified concrete storage process.

Credit rates for eligible expenditures

To qualify for the regular ITC rates described in the table below for property prepared or installed on or after November 28, 2023, a claimant must elect (in prescribed form and manner) to meet certain labour requirements regarding prevailing wages and apprenticeship requirements, for each qualified CCUS project. Otherwise, the credit rate will be reduced by 10 percentage points.

If a claimant elects to meet the labour requirements but fails to do so, the claimant is required to pay related penalties, which may be greater if the claimant does not take corrective measures. In certain circumstances, the claimant may lose its entitlement to the credit at the regular tax credit rate.

Refer to: Labour requirements for investment tax credits


Type of expenditure Incurred from 2022 to 2030 Incurred from 2031 to 2040
Qualified carbon capture expenditures incurred to capture carbon directly from ambient air 60% 30%
Qualified carbon capture expenditures incurred to capture carbon other than directly from ambient air 50% 25%
Qualified carbon transportation, storage, or use expenditures 37.5% 18.75%

How to calculate the credit

A claimant’s CCUS ITC is equal to the total of both:

  • the amount by which the claimant's cumulative CCUS development tax credit for the year exceeds its cumulative CCUS development tax credit for the immediately preceding taxation year
  • the claimant's CCUS refurbishment tax credit for the year

A claimant's cumulative CCUS development tax credit is the total of the specified percentage of each type of qualified CCUS expenditure incurred by the claimant in a taxation year and before the first day of commercial operations of the relevant qualified CCUS project. If the claimant is a member of a partnership, the cumulative CCUS development tax credit also includes the portion of the CCUS tax credit that can reasonably be considered to be its share of the credit.

A claimant’s CCUS refurbishment tax credit is the total of the specified percentage of each type of qualified CCUS expenditures incurred after the first day of commercial operations of the relevant qualified CCUS project. These expenditures on refurbishment are subject to a limit of 10% of total qualified CCUS expenditures incurred before the first day of commercial operations of the qualified CCUS project.

Recovery mechanism

Claimants must track and account for their actual use of captured CO2. If the actual eligible use percentage is lower than the projected eligible use percentage, they may have to repay credit amounts they have already received. In addition, claimants that dispose of property or export it from Canada may have to repay credit amounts.

How to claim the credit

Generally, you can claim the credit in the tax year in which the expenses are incurred, regardless of when the property becomes available for use.

Corporation

A corporation claiming the CCUS ITC must file their T2 Corporation Income Tax Return with the following:

  • Schedule 31, Investment Tax Credit - Corporations
    • The ITC claim for CCUS is at line 200 of Schedule 31
    • Include this amount on line 780 of the T2 Corporation Income Tax Return
  • Form that details how the ITC was calculated
    • This form will be available at a later date
    • Until this form is available, you must provide appropriate information with your return

Partnership

A partnership that allocates the CCUS ITC to its corporate members must:

  • Advise the amount of the credit allocated to each member
  • Provide a T5013 Statement of Partnership Income to each corporate member
  • File a form that details how the ITC was calculated with the Partnership Information Return for the fiscal year
    • This form will be available at a later date
    • Until this form is available, you must provide appropriate information with your return

A corporation that is a member of a partnership must claim the amount of the credit allocated from the partnership on:

  • Schedule 31, Investment Tax Credit – Corporations, with the T2 Corporation Income Tax Return
  • Form that details how the ITC was calculated
    • This form will be available at a later date
    • Until this form is available, you must provide appropriate information with your return

Provide appropriate information with your return

Until the form that details how the ITC was calculated becomes available, you must provide information with your return as described below.

What to provide with your return

Provide a summary of CCUS ITC claims in the year (total to line 200, Schedule 31) that includes:

  • Total CCUS ITC claims in the year from all CCUS projects summarized by each type of qualified expenditure
  • Total CCUS ITC claims allocated from a partnership for each CCUS ITC claim supported by Partnership Business Number (BN) and NRCan Reference number

For each qualified CCUS project, provide the following:

  • Cumulative CCUS Development ITC calculation information including:
    • Qualified CCUS expenditure by description for the year
    • Total qualified CCUS expenditure made in prior years identified by expenditure description
    • Total qualified CCUS expenditure made in current year identified by expenditure description
    • ITC rate applied to each qualified expenditure
    • Cumulative CCUS Development ITC by expenditure description
    • Cumulative CCUS Development ITC of the prior years by expenditure description
    • Current year CCUS ITC by expenditure description
    • Dual-use equipment must be identified separately for each qualified expenditure
  • Identification of any property disposition or export from Canada
  • Labour requirement election and attestation
  • Most recent Climate Risk Disclosure report production and disclosure date

Requirements to file reports

Corporations that have an ownership interest in a qualified CCUS projects that expects to have $20 million or more of qualified CCUS expenditures over the life of the qualified CCUS project (based on the most recent project evaluation or actual expenses) must produce and publish (in prescribed manner) an annual Climate Risk Disclosure report beginning with the tax year in which they first claim a CCUS tax credit through the first 20 years of operations of the qualified CCUS project.

A taxpayer that has claimed a CCUS ITC for a taxation year ending before the project start-up date of a qualified CCUS projects with qualified CCUS expenditures of $250 million or more over the life of the project (based on the most recent project evaluation issued by NRCan for the project, or based on actual expenditures incurred before the first day of commercial operations), will have to provide knowledge sharing reports to NRCan to be made publicly available.

Type of qualified expenditure

There are 5 types of qualified expenditures:

  • Qualified carbon capture expenditures (ambient air)
  • Qualified carbon capture expenditures (other)
  • Qualified carbon transportation expenditures
  • Qualified carbon storage expenditures
  • Qualified carbon use expenditures
