Clean Technology (CT) Investment Tax Credit (ITC)

The CT ITC is for taxable Canadian corporations or for partnerships in which a taxable Canadian corporation is a partner.

It is a refundable tax credit of up to 30% of the capital costs of clean technology property acquired and that becomes available for use from March 28, 2023, to December 31, 2034.

The CT ITC is intended to encourage investment of capital in the adoption and operations of new clean technology property in Canada.

Who can claim

Taxable Canadian corporations or partnerships in which a taxable Canadian corporation is a partner may be eligible for this investment tax credit.

What projects qualify

Eligible clean technology property includes:

  • zero-emission power generation technologies (including concentrated solar energy equipment and small modular nuclear reactors)
  • storage equipment for zero-emission energy and non-road zero-emission vehicles
  • geothermal energy systems unless they are used for geothermal energy projects that will co-produce oil, gas or other fossil fuels

Refer to Natural Resources Canada’s guide for class 43.1 and 43.2 property.


In order to qualify, clean technology property:

  • must be equipment that is situated in and intended for use exclusively in Canada
  • must not have been previously used or acquired for use or lease, for any purpose before acquisition by the taxpayer

Claiming one or more ITCs

Businesses are able to claim both the CT ITC and the Atlantic investment tax credit, without reduction.

Businesses can claim only one ITC for the same eligible property. This means they cannot claim the Clean Technology ITC if they claim one of the following credits for the same property:

  • Clean Hydrogen ITC
  • Clean Electricity ITC
  • Clean Technology Manufacturing ITC
  • Carbon Capture, Utilization and Storage ITC

Businesses may claim multiple ITCs for the same project, if the project includes different types of eligible property.

Credit rates for the eligible expenditures

Date of eligible expenditure Credit rate if labour requirements are met Credit rate if labour requirements are not met
From March 28, 2023 to December 31, 2033 30% 20%
From January 1, 2034 to December 31, 2034 15% 5%

The credit rate is subject to labour requirements.

Labour requirements

Businesses must elect to meet certain labour requirements for prevailing wages and apprenticeship standards to benefit from the full credit rate of the ITC.

If businesses do not meet the labour requirements, the credit rate will be reduced by 10 percentage points.

Responsibility for satisfying the labour requirements is on the person claiming the credit or a partnership where at least one partner is claiming the CT ITC.

Refer to: Labour requirements for investment tax credits

Recapture mechanism

Recapture requires the following conditions:

  • clean technology property acquired in the particular year or in any of the preceding 20 calendar years
  • taxpayer became entitled to a CT ITC
  • the property be converted to a non-clean technology use, be exported from Canada or be disposed of

How to claim the credit

If you are a corporation

A corporation claiming the CT ITC must file their T2 Corporation Income Tax Return with the following:

  • Schedule 31, Investment Tax Credit – Corporations
    • The ITC claim for clean technology is at line 155 of Schedule 31. Include this amount on line 780 of the T2 Corporation Income Tax Return.
  • Form (which will be made available later)
If you are a partnership

The CT ITC can be allocated only to members of a partnership that are taxable Canadian corporations.

A partnership claiming the CT ITC must do the following:

  • advise the amount of the credit allocated to each member
  • file a form (which will be made available later)
  • provide a T5013 slip to each member

A corporation that is a member of a partnership must claim the amount of the credit allocated from the partnership in the following:

  • a form (which will be made available later)
  • Schedule 31
